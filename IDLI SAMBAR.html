Idly Sambar
⌚ 40 mins
♨ Medium
★ ★ ★ ★ ☆ 4
🍽 Servings: 4

Play with your food! Adjust amounts based on your tastes.


INGREDIENTS

  1. 2 cup Idli Rice
  2. 0.50 cup Urad Dal
  3. 0.50 tsp Fenugreek Seeds
  4. 1 tsp Salt

METHOD

STEP 1:
Wash urad dal once and soak in water for at least 4 hours. Wash idli rice (parboiled rice) 2 - 3 times and soak rice and fenugreek seeds together in water for at least 4 hours. 

STEP 2:
After four hours discard the water from soaked urad dal and add the urad dal in a Blendtec jar. Add cold water little by little and grind it to a smooth batter. Choose batter option and grind for 3 - 4 times until the batter is smooth and fluffy. Transfer the batter to a wide bowl and set aside until needed.

STEP 3:
Strain and discard the water from soaked rice and add rice to a blendtec jar. Grind the rice to a smooth batter (little coarse batter is fine) by adding cold water little by little. Choose batter option for 2 - 3 times and grind until smooth. Transfer the rice batter to a wide bowl with urad dal batter, add salt and mix well with hand.

STEP 4:
Cover the bowl with a lid and set the batter for 8 hours or overnight for fermentation. After fermentation, the batter will rise in its quantity and the texture of the batter is light and foamy.

STEP 5:
Mix the batter once with ladle and add water if the batter is too thick. Now the idly batter is ready. 

STEP 6:
Grease the idli molds with oil and pour a spoonful of batter in each mold and steam the idlis in Idli cooker for 10 - 12 minutes or until you hear the whistle sound. 

STEP 7:
Let the idlis in the idly cooker itself for 2 - 3 minutes and transfer the idlies to a serving plate using a spoon. Hot steamed idlis are ready to be served.

INGREDIENTS FOR THE SAMBAR

  1. cup - tur dal, washed and soaked
  2. 3 cups - mixed vegetables of 1-inch size each (carrots, French beans, drumsticks, potatoes)
  3. salt to taste
  4. 1-1.5 inch - ball of black tamarind, seedless, soaked in 1 cup hot water
  5. 1/2 tsp - haldi/turmeric powder
  6. 1 tsp - red chilli powder
  7. 4 tbsp - `sambar` masala
  8. 1/2 tsp - hing/asafoetida
  9. 1 - chopped ripe tomato
  10. Then add the remaining half of all the powders and the coriander powder1/2 tsp - mustard seeds
  11. 1 sprig - curry leaves
  12. 1/2 tsp - jeera/cumin seeds
  13. 2-3 - dry red chillies
  14. 1 tsp - coriander/dhaniya powder
  15. salt to taste
  16. 2-3 tbsp - ghee/oil

METHOD FOR THE SAMBAR

STEP 1:
Boil the dal, mixed vegetables, and tomatoes after adding salt in a pressure cooker with just enough water to cover them. Cook up to 1 whistle on high flame.3

STEP 2:
Open the cooker and reduce the flame to simmer.

STEP 3:
Add half (each) of the turmeric, red-chilli powder, asafoetida, and sambar powder.

STEP 4:
Strain the tamarind pulp into this mixture. Allow to simmer.

STEP 6:
In a pan, heat the oil and add mustard seeds.

STEP 7:
When they splutter, reduce the flame and add the curry leaves, cumin, and red chillies.

STEP 8:
Keep stirring, and add a little water to prevent burning at the base. Fry for 15 seconds.

STEP 9:
Add this to the simmering curry and let it cook for 5 minutes.
